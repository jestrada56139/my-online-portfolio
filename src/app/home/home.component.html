Joining the SEAL's

Quick Run Down

Towards the middle of Winter, ending of January, I was able to paricipate in the SEAL's program by TxT. In order to be able to participate, you must have completed at least one summer with TxT. I wanted to learn more about code and myself so I decided to attempt to join it, and I did. Meeting 3 times a week, where Tuesday's and Saturday's are for Tech and Thursdays being for Leadership. Below are some of my journals during my venture.

1.31.18

Today markes the second day of the SEAL program. Yesterday, we went over expectations and what we should expect, as well what they expect. I really enjoyed the first challenge given to us: Reverse Challenge. Taking a user input and reversing it to spell it backwards. Because I see the challenge more of a puzzle that I wan't to solve. What really drives me is to know when there is less information, but to utilize what you have. However, I have a habit of not asking for help because I don't know how to. Putting me in an awkard state of inefficieny. So in the future I am going to ask for help if I get stuck.

2.8.18

Today was very interesting. It was a leadership day today. We were in charge of writing a 3 page paper about "What is Leadership?" What was interesting was that I took some actions as me being challenged. Understanding the fact that I make assumptions when someone asks me a certain question that can be viewed differently upon others, I take the the negative connotation, even if there wasn't one. From the beginning, I always views the class as being right or wrong and Oscar being the one who teaches us. But in reality, it's just a conversation between two people, or better all of us. I also learned what it meant to think critically, to me it is a skill that I have yet to debelop but I want to be able to use it in any instance. Not accepting the norm but to instead question why it happened, the logical stance behind it. I really think I am building myself to be better and am really enjoying the SEAL's.
